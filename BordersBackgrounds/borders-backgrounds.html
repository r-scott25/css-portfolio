Borders and Backgrounds

Border Syntax

  • Individual Properties
    • border-top-style: solid;
    • border-right-style: solid;
    • border-bottom-style: solid;
    • border-left-style: solid;

    • border-top-width: 1px;
    • border-right-width: 1px;
    • border-bottom-width: 1px;
    • border-left-width: 1px;

    • border-top-color: black;
    • border-right-color: black;
    • border-bottom-color: black;
    • border-left-color: black;
  • Shorthand Notation
    • border-top: 1px solid black;
    • border-right: 1px solid black;
    • border-bottom: 1px solid black;
    • border-left: 1px solid black;

    • If all sides will be the same, this shorthand can be condensed even more:
    • border: 1px solid black;

Note:

  • Borders do affect the overall width of an element.
  • Border widths begin at the edge of padding widths and extend outward
  • Example: .element {width: 100px; padding: 20px; border: 5px;} -- The total width of this element is 100 + 20 + 20 + 5 + 5 = 150px

Border Color

  • Allows you to define a specific color for your border that's independent of an element's foreground and background color
  • border-color: green;

Border vs. Outline

  • An outline is drawn just outside of the border's edge.
  • Outlines are important for usability and accessability
  • Outlines
    • They always go around all the sides. (You can't specify particular sides like you can with a border)
    • They're not part of the box model, so they won't affect the position of the element or adjacent elements
    • Avoid setting an outline to 0 or none. This will remove the browser's default focus style. If an element can be interacted with, it needs a visible focus indicator

Background

  • Individual Properties
    • background-attachment: //value;
    • background-clip: //value;
    • background-color: //value;
    • background-image: //value;
    • background-origin: //value;
  • Shorthand Notation
    • background is shorthand CSS, letting you define all background style properties at once, such as color, image, size, etc.
    • background: url(text.jpg) no-repeat center;
    • The default style for most elements is to have a transparent background - you can see through them.
    • By explicitly setting the background property, you can choose to display solid colors, gradients, or even images behind an element's content.
  • When specifying a background image, versus a color, you can control its position whether it repeats along an axis, the image size, and more.
    • backgroun-image: url(star.jpg);
    • backgroun-position: left;
    • backgroun-repeat: repeat-x;
    • backgroun-size: 200px;
  • Backgrounds can have both a background color and a background image
    • Background colors are layered underneath background images
